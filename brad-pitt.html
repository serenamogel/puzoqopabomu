Who is Brad Pitt?

Brad Pitt w as born on December 18, 1963, in Shawnee, Oklahoma, to trucking company owner William Alvin Pitt and school counselor Jane Etta. His family quickly relocated to Springfield, Missouri, where he resided with his younger siblings, Douglas Mitchell (born 1966) and Julie Neal (born 1969). He was raised as a Southern Baptist and then “oscillated[d] between agnosticism and atheism” after being born into a fundamentalist Christian household. He later reconciled his spiritual ideas. Pitt grew up in Springfield, which he describes as “Mark Twain country, Jesse James country,” with “a lot of hills, a lot of lakes.”

Pitt went to Kickapoo High School and participated in the golf, swimming, and tennis teams. He was a member of the Key and Forensics clubs at school, as well as school debates and musicals. After graduating from high school in 1982, Pitt enrolled at the University of Missouri, majoring in journalism with a focus on advertising. Pitt was not ready to settle down as graduation approached. He enjoyed movies—”a portal into different worlds for me”—and, since they were not manufactured in Missouri, he wanted to go to where they were made.

Pitt left the university two weeks before finishing his degree courses and traveled to Los Angeles, where he took acting classes and worked odd jobs. He cites Gary Oldman, Sean Penn, and Mickey Rourke as early acting influences.

How many Philanthropy works has Brad Pitt supported?

Pitt, a man of many abilities, has a keen interest in architecture, particularly in global efforts to develop environmentally friendly structures through sustainable architecture and design. This prompted him to organize a group of specialists in New Orleans to explore large-scale green, affordable housing construction to assist those families most in need following Hurricane Katrina’s devastation. That same group is now formally known as The Make It Right Foundation, a nonprofit organization dedicated to financing and building 150 new houses in New Orleans’ Ninth Ward, rebuilding lives and communities with a focus on sustainability and affordability.

The first six residences were finished in October 2008. Pitt met with US President Barack Obama and House Speaker Nancy Pelosi in March 2009, proposing his vision of “green housing” as a national model and discussing the possibility of public assistance.

Pitt won an award from the U.S. Green Building Council, a non-profit trade organization that promotes sustainability in the design, construction, and operation of buildings, in September 2009, in honor of the green housing idea employed for the Make It Right foundation.

His consistent devotion to humanitarian issues around the world has led him to demonstrate his heart by donating time and resources to numerous charities. According to tax records, Pitt and Angelina Jolie donated more than $8 million to charity in 2006.

Also, Pitt and Jolie traveled to Pakistan in November 2005 to examine the aftermath of the Kashmir earthquake. They flew to Haiti the following year to visit a school supported by The Yéle Hati Foundation, a charity founded by Haitian-born hip hop musician Wyclef Jean that uses music, sports, and the media to reinforce projects that are making a difference in education, health, the environment, and community development. Pitt and Jolie donated to three aid organizations in Chad and Darfur affected by the Darfur crisis in May 2007.
