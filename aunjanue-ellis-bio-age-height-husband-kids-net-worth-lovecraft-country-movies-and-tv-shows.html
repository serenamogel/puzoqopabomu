Aunjanue Ellis Biography

Aunjanue Ellis is an American Film, Stage, and Television Actress and Producer, who was born in San Francisco, California and brought up in Magnolia, Mississippi, United States of America. She is widely known for her roles in the HBO drama series ‘Lovecraft Country’ as Hippolyta Freeman, the period drama film ‘The Help’ as Yule May Davis, the horror drama series ‘True Blood’ as Diane, the musical drama film ‘Ray’ as Mary Ann Fisher, and in the CBS action series ‘NCIS: Los Angeles’ as Michelle Hanna.

    Aunjanue Ellis Career

    Her first professional film was as Ariel in the Broadway revival, The Tempest. Later on, Aunjanue got the role in the drama series, New York Undercover. Ellis had the co-leading role in the film, Girls Town, in 1996 alongside Lili Taylor. Ellis has continued to have several roles in different movies and Television shows.

    In 2014, Ellis was cast as a lead character in the international co-production epic miniseries The Book of Negroes, based on Lawrence Hill’s bestselling 2007 novel. The Book of Negroes premiered in 2015, and Ellis received critical acclaim for her performance. The Hollywood Reporter critic Whitney Matheson praised her performance, wrote in review “Ellis’ gripping performance that holds the six-part miniseries together.

    Except for the first installment that focuses on Aminata’s girlhood, Ellis is present in nearly every scene, aging decades and displaying a stunning range of emotion.” Ellis received Critics’ Choice Television Award for Best Actress in a Movie or Miniseries nomination for her performance.

    On February 25, 2015, it was announced that Ellis was cast as a character in the ABC thriller series, Quantico. She left the series after two seasons in 2017. In 2020, Aunjanue was cast in King Richard, a biopic about Richard Williams. Ellis plays opposite Will Smith. Also, Ellis plays the role of Mattie Moss Clark, Clark Sisters’ mother, in the film, The Clark Sisters: The First Ladies of Gospel.

    Aunjanue Ellis Lovecraft Country

    Additionally, in June 2018, Aunjanue was cast in the upcoming drama series, Lovecraft Country. It is based on the novel of Lovecraft Country by Matt Ruff. It is a drama horror television series. The series is produced by Monkeypaw Productions, Bad Robot Productions, and Warner Bros.
